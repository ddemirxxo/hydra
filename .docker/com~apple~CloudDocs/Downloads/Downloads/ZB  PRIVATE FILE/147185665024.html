Magnets

I’ve always been told I am a magnet for trouble.

If you leave me alone in a room full of people I am pretty much guaranteed to find me surrounded very quickly by the ones in the room society would consider ‘trouble’

They usually have a shitty past, tormented childhood. All consist of some legitimate but usually unspoken justification as to why they self medicate with illicit or pharmaceutical medication. Have everyday struggles or have been misdiagnosed somewhere along their life span with depression or anxiety by some doctor they met once, the doctor that your mum speaks so highly of and all her friends go too.
The doctor she dragged you to see when you still lived at home and lived by rules and a set of standards you thought the rest of the world lived by.
Before you learnt that your parents, their friends and every person on this vast planet are just a product of what they were taught, what they were told. Right and wrong. Heaven and hell. The rules.

But you never agreed with the rules. Your parents thought you were disobedient and disrespectful. The doctor said it was and or bipolar and of course depression, anxiety. Your school told your parents you were a distraction to all the other more promising students, and your friends were either drawn to you or repelled.
